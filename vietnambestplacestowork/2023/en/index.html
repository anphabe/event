Ms. THANH VU

The first Asia woman to complete the 4 Deserts Grand Slam
The first Asia woman to win the IUTA World Champion
Running on all seven continents all over the world

Ms. Thanh Vu graduated from Schulich School of Business, Canada and worked at Bloomberg (Singapore). With a passion for running, she set aside her career in financial analysis to train as a professional endurance runner.

In 2016, she set a record with the achievement of being the first Asian woman to complete the 4 Deserts Grand Slam (totaling 1000km), running through the world's most challenging deserts (Namibia, Gobi, Atacama, and Antarctica). Subsequently, she continuously conquered various highly demanding races, including The Track, a 522km race in Australia, a 230km race in the European Arctic Circle, a 273km race across the Grand Canyon in North America (one of the 7 natural wonders), and the Everest Trail Race in the Himalayas, etc. In 2022, she accomplished the challenge of running on all seven continents and became the first Asian woman to the IUTA World Champion of the Deca Ultra Triathlon distance in the continuous format, which spans over 38km swim, 1800km bike and 422km run.

She is hailed as the "Desert Queen" inspiring communities to overcome personal limitations and strive to become the best version of themselves, bringing Vietnamese pride to the world.
